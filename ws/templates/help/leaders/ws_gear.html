Individual Safety Gear (All Participants)

Essentials

  • Clothing
  • Footwear
  • Food & water
  • Whistle
  • Compass
  • Map (photocopy provided by leader is sufficient)
  • Headlamp or flashlight

Above-treeline Gear

  • Goggles
  • Balaclava. When worn together, goggles + balaclava should leave no exposed skin

Traction/Floatation

  • Crampons
  • Microspikes
  • Snowshoes
  • Skis

Special Gear

Depending on your trip, there may be some specialized gear that should be brought along. For example:

  • Skis
  • Fatbikes
  • Ice tools
  • Etc.

Group Gear

Trip leaders must ensure that collectively their group carries the following gear according to trip type.

A Trip

  • MITOC first-aid kit
  • Extra clothing
  • Extra food
  • Duct tape
  • Parachute cord or webbing
  • Foam pad (optional)

B Trip

Everything for an A Trip plus ...

  • 2 winter sleeping bags rated for the forecasted low temp of the night at the elevations you expect to travel to (check on Mountain Forecast)
  • 1 foam pad for every 2 people
  • Winter-rated stove with pot, lid, fuel (min 22oz, 33oz recommended)
  • 2 types of fire starters (flint + steel, waterproof matches, lighter)
  • 2 Bivvies
  • 2 Outdoor Research Alpine bivvies
    • Or one MITOC pack of 3 large yellow plastic bags
    • Or one extra shelter
  • Shelter/Tent
    • MITOC beta-mid (2 person pyramid tarp)
    • Or MITOC beta-light (2 person pyramid tarp)
    • Or MITOC mega-light (4 person pyramid tarp)
    • Or MITOC bothy bag (Terra Nova Bothy 8)
    • Or your own 3-season tent

C Trips

Everything for a B Trip plus...

  • Mountaineering Shelter/Tent: BD First Light, MHW Direkt 2, Trango, or other 4-season mountaineering tent (instead of B Trip shelter)
  • Shovel (optional)
  • Wands (optional)

Skiing Trip

Everything for a B or C Trip plus...

  • Ski repair kit (at least a multi-tool)
  • Shovel (optional)

Overnight Trip

Everything for a C Trip plus...

  • Shovel
  • Adequate shelter for everyone - don't overstuff tents
