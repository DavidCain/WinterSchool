Individual Safety Gear (All Participants)

Essentials

  • Clothing
  • Footwear
  • Food & water
  • Whistle
  • Compass
  • Map (photocopy provided by leader is sufficient)
  • Headlamp or flashlight

Above-treeline Gear

  • Goggles
  • Balaclava. When worn together, goggles + balaclava should leave no exposed skin

Traction/Floatation

  • Crampons
  • Microspikes
  • Snowshoes
  • Skis

Special Gear

Depending on your trip, there may be some specialized gear that should be brought along. For example:

  • Skis
  • Fatbikes
  • Ice tools
  • Etc.

Group Safety Gear

Trip leaders must ensure that collectively their group carries the following gear according to trip type.

A Trip

  • One MITOC first aid kit,
  • Extra layers (clothing),
  • Extra food and water,
  • Two foam pads,
  • Duct tape or climbing tape, and
  • Parachute cord or webbing.

B Trip

  • All the group gear required for an A Trip plus ...
  • Two sleeping bags (one winter (15F) rated and one three-season (35F) rated minimum),
  • One bivy or bothy bag - A large MITOC tarp, an actual bothy bag, or a bivy bag such as the OR Alpine Bivy),
  • One shelter - A three-season or four-season tent and if weather conditions permit a bothy or tarp, and
  • A winter-rated stove with pot, lid, fuel, and fire starters (optional).

C Trips

  • All the group gear required for a B Trip with the following modifications ...
  • Two winter-rated (rated 15F or better) sleeping bags (instead of the one winter and one three-season bag for a B Trip), and
  • One shelter - A four season tent or mountaineering tent.

Overnight Trip

For overnight trips the safety gear must consist of

  • Everything for a C Trip plus...
  • Two shovels, and
  • Adequate shelter for everyone - don't overstuff tents.

Group Safety Gear - Ice Climbing Trips

On Ice Climbing Trips, leaders must ensure that collectively their group carries the following gear according to trip rating.

AI Trip

  • One MITOC first aid kit,
  • Extra layers (clothing),
  • Extra food and water,
  • One foam pads,
  • Duct tape or climbing tape, and
  • Parachute cord or webbing.

While the following items may be left in the car

  • One winter-rated sleeping bag,
  • One bivy or bothy bag,
  • One shelter, and
  • A winter-rated stove with pot, lid, fuel, and fire starters (optional).

BI Trip

For BI Trips all AI Trip gear is required and none of it may be left in the car.

CI Trip

For CI Trips the WSC requires that trip leaders provide a detailed list of safety gear specific to the proposed trip objectives and associated hazards.
