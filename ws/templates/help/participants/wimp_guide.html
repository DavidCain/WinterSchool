How to be the WIMP

Responsibilities of the WIMP

As the "Worry if Missing Person," it is the job of the WIMP to ensure that all trips return safely, and to coordinate an emergency response if a trip is unaccounted for. During Winter School, the WIMP is a volunteer position filled each week by a volunteer MITOCer.

Expectations

In order to serve as the WIMP, you must not be a participant on any other trips for the weekend. You should be easily reached by phone at any time over the weekend. You must also being willing to coordinate a rescue if needed.

Timeline

  1. Distribute your contact information to the leaders by Thursday.
  2. As the WIMP, you are able to see a summary of trip itineraries on the "WIMP" tab.
  3. On Friday, make sure all trips have submitted itineraries and driver information, and send reminder emails to the leaders if necessary.
  4. Make a list of all the trips out and their worry times, preferably in chronological order. Keep this list handy for the weekend.
    • You can also print the summary of trip itineraries, but note that medical information will automatically be omitted.
    • Do not print or email participant medical information.
  5. Check off trips as they report back.
  6. When all of the day's trips are back safely, consider sending an email to the WSC.

What to do if a trip does not report back before its worry time

  1. Try calling the leaders.
  2. Wait 30 minutes, then call the leaders again. If they don't respond, call each of the participants.
  3. Repeat every 30 minutes.
  4. Also try calling someone at each of the cabins to see if the trip has returned but failed to inform the WIMP.

If no contact is made 2 hours after the worry time (5 hours after the expected return time), then call the appropriate emergency number.

  • For trips in NH, contact the NH State Police at 800-525-5555 or 911.
  • For trips in VT, contact the VT State Police by calling the nearest state police station or 911.
  • For trips in ME, contact the ME State Police by calling the nearest state police station or 911.
  • For trips in MA, contact the MA State Police by calling the nearest regional dispatch or 911.
